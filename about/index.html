About

July 23, 2018 · 4 minute read

TL;DR

My very first interest on computers dates from my teenage years, when the hard drive from my first PC died. I was 12 years old, and while trying to get some fix to my computer, I felt myself ashmed because someone told me that “the issue is the hard drive and the fix will cost you this!” and I could not do anything about it … because I did not know what a Hard Drive were or meant …

Since that day, several situations related to computers intrigued me a lot, aways caming to me like a karma … From the obscure DOS commands to several floppy disks needed to install a new game; from the unreliable dial-up internet connection of the end of 90s to computer viruses and Win98 re-installations … I knew inside that I needed to learn more about all of that.

When I was 15, I started a two-years technical course on Electronics / Electrictian, and I get a chance to learn the very basic foundations on how computers are made : I learned about boolean algebra, transistors, digital circuits, something about microprocessors, and I even “programmed” for the very first time using PLCs. However, I still did not understand how my PC at home really works … And I knew that I still had more to learn.

At 18 I started my very first bachelor course at UNICAMP on Mathematics. I gave up in first period, because I felt that Computer Science should be my way, and I was not able to qualify to CS at the admission tests (called “Vestibular” in Brazil) … I love Math until today, and after trying again and again, I had TONS of math courses at USP, the university where I gratuated on Computer Science (several years later) and the instituition I own all my formal background, and much more.

While studying Computer Science - in a fulltime course - ironically, I did not see myself as a fulltime programmer, anyday; I quite disliked programming at my first CS days, and I had much more interest on computer infrastructure knowledge and courses, like Networks, Operational Systems, Computer Architecture and so on … My first internship - dealing with front-end development of our library website - using technologies like JQuery and Javascript circa 2008 did not help either in this context.

But, in 2009 the world was totally hyped about smartphones, and bought my first Macbook as well. I started to learn about iPhone programming - trying to grasp objective-C with my naive C background - and when the first iPad was launched, I realized that Mobile could become “the next Ruby on Rails” to focus and learn. I remember that several friends from that days picked up the technical carrer as a developer with Web development, while I started to learn Android as well, once we had a strong Java background from CS courses (I and could not afford an iPhone at that time)

I have been doing software development for Mobile platforms professionaly since 2010. I work both with Android and iOS, but since 2013, I decided to become an Android specialist; I deep dived on this ecossystem, and offered this knowledge for several successful products on the perspective of Brazillian market, like VivaReal, Magazine Luiza and Stone Pagamentos.

I worked for several industries across dozen of projects, from content media apps to e-commerce, and I faced several different challenging problems across all of them, learning a lot. I have been working with backend development, and nowadays I’m 100% interested in the backend landscape since we have Kotlin as new shining option on this field, and we have technologies like Serverless as well.

I’m a father of a beautiful boy, who lives in Brazil with her mother. At the spare time, I like to learn a little bit more about philoshopy, sociology and other human sciences. As a computer nerd, I enjoy the most of the usual geek stuff as well, but I’m not into games for no special reason …

At the end of the day, I see myself just like a curious guy, and a hardworker person instead of a super-clever one.
